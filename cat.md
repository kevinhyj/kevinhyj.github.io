        <h2>Two almost-mine cats.</h2>
        <p>My family had cats twice before, both rescued strays. My parents did most of the raising, so I never fully count them as mine. Still, they left very real paw prints in my memory.</p>
      </div>

      <div class="cat-memory-feature">
        <img src="{{ '/assets/cat/memories/lai-fu.jpg' | relative_url }}" alt="Lai Fu, the first rescued family cat">
        <div class="cat-memory-copy">
          <p class="eyebrow">Lai Fu · 来福</p>
          <h3>Lai Fu (来福), which honestly sounds like a dog name.</h3>
          <p>He came home the day before one of my final exams. That evening I lay on my bedroom floor and talked to him, absurdly happy. The next day I ranked first in that exam, so maybe Lai Fu was my first academic collaboration.</p>
        </div>
      </div>

      <div class="cat-litter-story">
        <div class="cat-litter-copy">
          <p class="eyebrow">Xiao Maqiu · 小麻球</p>
          <h3>Winter kittens on the car hood.</h3>
          <p>Another time, in winter, a mother cat placed her kittens on the hood of our family car to borrow the engine's warmth.</p>
          <p>The white kitten was originally called Xiao Maqiu (小麻球). He got sick and left for another world far too early, so his black brother inherited the name. I always thought 小麻球 sounded like the name of a black cat anyway. In a strange little way, the name found its right owner.</p>
        </div>
